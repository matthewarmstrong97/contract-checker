Common traps we flag

"Pay When Paid" Clauses

You don't get paid until they do. If the client goes bust, you're last in line.

🔒

Retention Traps

Your money locked for months with no clear release date or conditions.

📐

Vague Scope

If scope isn't specific, you end up doing extra work for free.

⚡

Liability Traps

You pay for things outside your control — damage caused by others on site.

Delayed Payment Terms

DO NOT SIGN

Two clauses expose you to unlimited personal liability and the payment terms likely violate the Construction Contracts Act 2002.

🚨 CRITICAL ISSUES
Issue 1
Unlimited Liability Clause (Clause 14.3)
If anything goes wrong on site, you're personally liable with no cap. The head contractor can pursue you for the full project value — even for damage caused by other trades. On a $500k project, this clause could wipe you out.

Financial impact: Potentially unlimited — could exceed the value of your subcontract many times over.
NZ law: Contract and Commercial Law Act 2017.
💬 Say this:
"I'm happy to accept liability for my own work, but I need a cap on clause 14.3 — let's set it at the value of my subcontract. That's standard practice. Can we add that wording?"
Issue 2
Pay When Paid (Clause 8.1)
You only get paid when the head contractor receives payment from the client. The NZ Construction Contracts Act 2002 makes pay-when-paid clauses unenforceable in most cases — but you'd need to challenge it.

Financial impact: On a $60k job with 90-day client terms, you could be out of pocket for 3+ months — roughly $3,500 in financing costs.
💬 Say this:
"Clause 8.1 looks like a pay-when-paid clause, which conflicts with my rights under the CCA 2002. I need payment within 20 working days of my invoice, regardless of when you're paid. Can we change this?"
⚠️ THINGS TO WATCH
Issue 3
Retention — No Release Date (Clause 11.2)
5% retention with no specified release date beyond "practical completion at the head contractor's discretion."

Financial impact: On a $60k subcontract, that's $3,000 held indefinitely.
💬 Say this:
"Can we add a specific release date to clause 11.2? I'd like 50% at practical completion and the remainder 12 months after."
✅ LOOKS FINE
  • Scope of work is clearly defined in Schedule A — no ambiguity
  • Insurance requirements are standard (public liability $2M, Building Act 2004)
  • Dispute resolution follows standard NZ construction practice
  • H&S obligations align with WorkSafe NZ guidelines
❓ QUESTIONS TO ASK BEFORE SIGNING
  • Who is the end client and what are their payment terms to you?
  • Can we agree a specific practical completion date in writing?
  • Has clause 14.3 been negotiated with other subcontractors on this project?
📋 PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY

This contract has two serious problems. The liability clause has no cap and the payment clause likely violates the CCA 2002. The scope and insurance sections are fine. Push back on clauses 14.3 and 8.1 before proceeding.

📌 WHAT TO DO NOW
  1. Do not sign this contract in its current form.
  2. Send the negotiation scripts above to the head contractor and request changes in writing.
  3. If they refuse to negotiate, consider walking away or consulting a construction lawyer before proceeding.
