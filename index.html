Common traps we flag
💰

"Pay When Paid" Clauses

You don't get paid until they do. If the client goes bust or disputes, you're last in line.

🔒

Retention Traps

Your money locked for months with no clear release date or process.

📐

Vague Scope

You end up doing extra work for free because the scope was never pinned down.

⚡

Liability Traps

You pay for things outside your control — clauses that expose you for other trades' work.

📅

Delayed Payment Terms

60 or 90-day payment terms buried in the fine print. Serious cashflow problems.

🚨 Critical Issues
Unlimited Liability Clause (Clause 14.3) If anything goes wrong on site, you're personally liable with no cap. The head contractor can come after you for the full project value — even for problems that weren't your fault.
"I'm happy to accept liability for my own work, but I need a cap — let's set it at the value of my subcontract. Can we add that to clause 14.3?"
Pay When Paid (Clause 8.1) You only get paid when the head contractor gets paid by the client. Under the NZ Construction Contracts Act 2002, this clause may be unenforceable — but you'd have to fight for it.
"Clause 8.1 looks like a pay-when-paid clause which conflicts with my rights under the CCA 2002. Can we change it to net-30 from invoice date?"
💸 What This Could Cost You
  • Liability clause: potentially unlimited — could exceed the contract value
  • Late payment: up to 90 days on a $80k job = $3,600+ in financing costs
  • Vague scope: could mean 20–30 hours of uncosted extra work
